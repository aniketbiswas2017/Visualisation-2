ABOUT GLOBAL TERRORISM

“Global Terrorism is the deliberate creation and exploitation of fear through violence or the threat of violence in the pursuit of political change” - Bruce Hoffman, Inside Terrorism

There have been 1,70,000 cases of terrorist attacks around the world recorded from the year 1970 to the year 2016 and the numbers are rising exponentially.

The National Consortium for the Study of Terrorism and Responses to Terrorism (START) maintains an open-source dataset known as Global Terrorism Database (GTD), which contains information of all terrorist attacks ever recorded. The data has been collected from unclassified sources and and is composed of 100 different variables and attributes.

ABOUT THIS PROJECT

The main aim of this project is to highlight the important aspects of all these attacks that has helped shape neoteric history and constitute modern civilization that we know today.

This project aims at discovering patterns and trends of attacks while providing an informative overview of the number of attacks.

A number of interesting phenomenon were observed during the course of this project. There is an obvious trend as to the number of incidents growing in countries that gained independence in the latter half of the 20th century as opposed to the already developed countries.

There is also rising tendency and gradual shift of the number of attacks from the Western Hemisphere to the African, Middle Eastern and Asian regions near the equator. This also signifies that the developed countries has fought an uphill battle in mitigating similar types of attacks.

ABOUT THE VISUALISATION

The first visualisation is referred to as "TIMELINE". It displays the major terrorist attacks over the years and the gradual shift of incidents from the Western Hemisphere to the Eastern Hemisphere.

The second visualisation is labeled "CASUALTIES". It shows the number of attacks, the number of lives lost and the average (success) rate of all recorded incidents by attack type, with the help of a pie chart.

The third visualisation has been termed as "PERPETRATORS". It exposes the major designated terrorist organizations that are directly or indirectly responsible for the loss of lives of millions of people on earth.
